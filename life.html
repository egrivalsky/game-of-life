Conway's Game of Life

What's this all about?

The game of live as described by John Conway in 1970 can be summarized by three rules:

  1. Any live cell with two or three live neighbours survives.
  2. Any dead cell with three live neighbours becomes a live cell.
  3. All other live cells die in the next generation. Similarly, all other dead cells stay dead.
This board is not infinite and there for not a perfect GoL board, but it's fun to play with and it was fun to make. More information about John Conway's Game of Life is on Wikipedia.
